The Starfish

by George Tragkas

One day, an old man was walking along a beach that was littered with thousands of starfish washed ashore.

As he walked, he noticed a young girl picking up starfish one by one and throwing them back into the ocean.

Curious, he asked her why she was doing this. The girl replied, "The sun is up, and the tide is going out.

If I don't throw them back, they will die." The old man smiled and said, "But there are so many. How can you make a difference?"

The girl picked up another starfish, gently tossed it into the water, and said, "I made a difference for that one."

This story reminds us that even small acts of kindness and compassion can bring hope and make a positive impact in the world.

- adapted from "The Star Thrower" by Loren C. Eiseley
