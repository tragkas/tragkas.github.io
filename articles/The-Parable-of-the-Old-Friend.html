The Parable of the Old Friend

by George Tragkas

One day, a man named James met with his old friend. They had not seen each other in ten years since they were both in high school. When they were younger, James spoke to his friend about partying and having fun. But now, James was 25 years old, and he spoke to his friend about his job and how much he hated it. He complained about how tedious and monotonous it was.

The friend listened patiently and then offered James some advice. He told James that if he didn't like his job, he should consider changing it and doing something he was passionate about. After all, life was short, and James had only a limited amount of time on this earth.

James was struck by the wisdom of his friend's words. He realized that he had been wasting his time and energy on a job he hated. He began to think about what he really wanted to do with his life, and he remembered a dream he had always had - to start his own business. He became excited at the prospect and told his friend about it.

A week later, James called his friend again, full of enthusiasm and eager to share his progress. But to his friend's disappointment, James had not made any progress on his business idea. Instead, he had gotten caught up in the petty drama of his job and was more concerned with complaining about his co-workers than making his dreams a reality.

The friend shook his head and thought to himself, "Oh, James." He realized that James had let his fear and insecurity hold him back from pursuing his passions and making the most of his life.
