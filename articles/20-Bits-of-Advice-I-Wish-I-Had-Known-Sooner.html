20 Bits of Advice I Wish I Had Known Sooner

by George Tragkas

I read 270 Bits of Advice, then I listed the best 20. Find out yours here (68), here (99) and here (103).

1. Learn how to learn from those you disagree with, or even offend you. See if you can find the truth in what they believe.

2. Extraordinary claims should require extraordinary evidence to be believed.

3. Everyone is shy. Other people are waiting for you to introduce yourself to them, they are waiting for you to send them an email, they are waiting for you to ask them on a date. Go ahead.

4. When you are young spend at least 6 months to one year living as poor as you can, owning as little as you possibly can, eating beans and rice in a tiny room or tent, to experience what your “worst” lifestyle might be. That way any time you have to risk something in the future you won’t be afraid of the worst case scenario.

5. The more you are interested in others, the more interesting they find you. To be interesting, be interested.

6. How to apologize: Quickly, specifically, sincerely.

7. When someone is nasty, rude, hateful, or mean with you, pretend they have a disease. That makes it easier to have empathy toward them which can soften the conflict.

8. What you get by achieving your goals is not as important as what you become by achieving your goals.

9. Recipe for success: under-promise and over-deliver.

10. It’s not an apology if it comes with an excuse. It is not a compliment if it comes with a request.

11. If you can avoid seeking approval of others, your power is limitless.

12. About 99% of the time, the right time is right now.

13. Don’t ever work for someone you don’t want to become.

14. Don’t keep making the same mistakes; try to make new mistakes.

15. Ask funders for money, and they’ll give you advice; but ask for advice and they’ll give you money.

16. The biggest lie we tell ourselves is “I don’t need to write this down because I will remember it.”

17. Your growth as a conscious being is measured by the number of uncomfortable conversations you are willing to have.

18. The best way to get a correct answer on the internet is to post an obviously wrong answer and wait for someone to correct you.

19. When introduced to someone make eye contact and count to 4. You’ll both remember each other.

20. You see only 2% of another person, and they see only 2% of you. Attune yourselves to the hidden 98%.

Enjoy.
