Our brain is like a computer

by Georgios Tragkas

1. Update it's software:

-Learn new things: Engaging in learning and new experiences can help to keep your brain "up to date" and improve your mental flexibility and problem-solving skills.

-Exercise: Physical activity has been shown to improve brain function and protect against age-related cognitive decline.

2. Clean it's hardware:

-Go for a hike or walk in nature. This can be a great way to disconnect from technology and enjoy the beauty of the natural world.

-Take up a hobby or craft. Painting, or woodworking are all activities that can be done offline and can be very rewarding.

-Practice mindfulness or meditation. Taking a few minutes to focus on your breath and clear your mind can help you feel more present and disconnected from technology.

3. Protect it's battery:

-Get enough sleep: Adequate sleep is important for maintaining brain health and function.

-Eat a healthy diet: A diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and other nutrients has been linked to better brain health and function.

-Detox from technology: free your mind from the world of information.

Enjoy.
